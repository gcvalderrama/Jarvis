Chronology surrounding the Exxon Valdez oil spill: March 24, the
tanker runs aground; March 30, the captain is fired; June 19, oil
companies announce plan for $250 million network of response teams;
September 15, beach cleanup operations suspended for winter; October
10, Alaska warns against eating shell fish from the area; December 4,
Transportation Secretary announces that cleanup cost Exxon $1.9
billion; December 31, 1,000 sea otters, 140 bald eagles and 36,500
seabirds reported dead; February 27, Exxon and subsidiary indicted;
and March 22, captain found guilty of negligent discharge of oil.
Exxon was ordered to resume the beach cleanup on May 1.
