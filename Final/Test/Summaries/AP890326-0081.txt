On Sunday, the Exxon Shipping Co. said that the third mate was in
command of the Exxon Valdez and the captain was not on the bridge when
it ran aground on Friday.  The clean-up efforts were continuing, while
an Exxon claims officer met with fishermen.  The governor of Alaska
declared Prince William Sound a disaster area.  More than 4 miles of
floating boom were in place and skimming boats were working.  One
hundred square miles are estimated to be affected.  The transfer of
oil to a second tanker resumed late Saturday and is expected to take
six days.
