T. K. Wetherel, Speaker of the Florida House of Representatives,
writes the Wall Street Journal that an assertion that Alexander
Hamilton would have supported term limits would be incorrect.  The
Constitutional Convention rejected term limits as did the first
Congress.  He added that Madison said members of Congress ...by
frequent reelections," ..."will be thoroughly masters of the public
business."

He concludes that not Federalists, but those out-of-power clamored for
term limits, adding that Robert Livingston, at the convention to
ratify the Constitution, said "The people are the best judges (of) who
should represent them...."
