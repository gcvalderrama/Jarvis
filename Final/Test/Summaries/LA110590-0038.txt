Douglas Wakiihuri, a Kenyan who trains in Japan, and Poland's Wanda
Panfil, who lives in Mexico, were the winners in Sunday's hot and
humid New York City Marathon.  Wakiihuri's time was 2:12:39.  Panfil's
time was 2:30:45. With Kim Jones of Spokane, Washington, finishing
second, only 5 seconds behind.  Favorites, Juma Ikangaa and Grete
Waitz, finished fourth.  Ikangaa had won with a course record last
year and Waitz had won the race nine times.  The first American
finisher was Gerry O'Hara of New York, who came in 29th.  This was
Wakiihuri's eighth marathon and fourth win.
