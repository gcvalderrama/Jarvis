The Reagan administration moved to deal with the worsening drought.
An interagency group has been appointed, although there was no
prediction as to what kind of relief might be authorized.  In 13
states, farmers have been authorized to harvest hay on idled cropland.
The administration may have to curb its export enhancement plan,
especially concerning soybeans.  Bankers that lend money to farmers
are worried about their borrowers' financial help.  Farm equipment
makers are concerned that sales could suffer.  Crop yields may be so
low that farmers would be strapped for cash and effects would ripple
throughout the farm economy.
