A USAF A-10 attack jet, assigned to the 10th Tactical Fighter Wing at
RAF Alconbury, crashed and burned about 25 miles north of Cambridge,
England, today at 9:53am, while on a routine training flight. The
pilot ejected.  Statistically, the A-10 Thunderbolt II is one of the
safest planes in the AF inventory. It has been used since 1977 and the
USAF currently has 700.

On Dec. 8th an A-10 crashed into the Tohono O'Odham Indian Reservation
in Tracy, Arizona, 80 miles west of Tucson. The pilot ejected
safely. On the same day, another A- 10 crashed into Remscheid,
Germany, killing five.
