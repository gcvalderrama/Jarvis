The following is a brief look at forest fires in the Western states.
Alaska: 2.1 million acres are smoldering but the fires are not active;
Idaho: 17 major blazes are underway, mostly threatening in Star Valley
and Eagle Bar; Montana: One large fire is burning in the Scapegoat
Wilderness, and two in the Absaroka-Beartooth Wilderness; Oregon: the
biggest forest fire is the Tepee Butte fire in the Wallowa-Whitman
National Forest; Utah: fires are under control near Pineview
Reservoir; Wyoming: fires are burning around Yellowstone Park at North
Fork and Clover-Mist; and Huck and Mink Creek in Bridger-Teton
National Forest.
