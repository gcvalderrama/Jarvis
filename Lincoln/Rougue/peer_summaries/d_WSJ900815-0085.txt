A US Census bureau survey on childcare and low-income family could provide fuel for legislation already in congress . According to Helen Bank a childcare expert with the Children 's Defense Fund , legislation now in Congress `` would help with cost and widen choice '' for low-income family . The result confirm that poor family pay a higher percentage of their family income for child care service than do higher income family . The survey show that an average of about 25 % of the lower-income family ' weekly resource are spent on childcare while only 6 % of higher-income family ' weekly earnings go to childcare .
