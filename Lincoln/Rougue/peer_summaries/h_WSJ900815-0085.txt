A new Census Bureau survey showing how child are cared for in the US increase concern about affordable childcare for low-income family and could bolster childcare legislation now in Congress . Childcare cost average 25 % of income for poor family and 6 % for the better off . The proportion of child in daycare doubled from 1977 to 1987 . Fewer relative are used a daycare provider , reflecting the increase in working woman . 66 % of preschooler are cared for in their home or the home of others . Many low-income woman would prefer the developmental benefit of center-based preschool but ca n't afford it .
